TED Talks Geopolitics & Media

Interview 01: Why most of the news doesn't get your attention

Accessed Date: June 15, 2026 Source Page: TED YouTube channel Publicized Date: May 20, 2026 Link: View Content
Summary

This interview is hosted by Helen Walters. She interviews Ian Bremer, a renowned political scientist and founder of the Eurasia Group and GZERO Media. The main concept of this video is how to find reliable news and understand world politics.

Ian talks about how modern news is becoming more biased and politicized. He explains how to get reliable information from world leaders by building long-term relationships and avoiding single points of view. Instead of reading short, emotionally charged social media headlines, he advises the general public to consume longer-form content, such as podcasts or in-depth articles.

My Opinion

I think this interview is very useful in today’s digital world. With so much fake and biased news on the internet, Ian’s advice on how to choose our news sources carefully is very important. I completely agree with his idea that we should spend more time understanding deeper issues rather than reacting quickly to short news headlines.

Reflective Development - What You Learned
  • Geopolitical: Relating to politics and international relations around the world.
  • Simpatico: Having similar views or getting along well with someone.
  • Kleptocracy: A society or government ruled by corrupt leaders who steal national wealth.
  • Commodification: Made into a basic, common product that is easily available.
TED Talks Self-Improvement

Interview 02: 5 Practical Ways to Take Control of Your Life

Accessed Date: June 15, 2026 Source Page: TED YouTube channel Link: View Content
Summary

In this TED video, the speaker is Jim VandeHei, a successful journalist and founder of companies like Politico and Axios. The main concept of his talk is that in order to be successful, we need to focus on what we can control.

His main rule is "You control yourself." He explains five practical ways to take control of our lives: controlling our daily choices, controlling our reactions by "doing the next right thing," controlling our reality by choosing positive information, controlling how others see us, and ultimately, controlling our destiny.

My Opinion

I think this video is very motivating. Jim’s message of “You are in control” is a good reminder for everyone. Instead of blaming our problems on bad luck or other people, we should take responsibility for our own actions. I especially liked his simple advice to “do the next right thing” because it makes it much easier to handle difficult situations.

Reflective Development - What You Learned
  • Doomscroll: Spending a lot of time reading negative news on social media or the internet.
  • Obsession: Being extremely focused or obsessed with doing something.
  • Self-indulgence: Doing exactly what you want or focusing entirely on yourself.
  • Editing: Content that provides good moral or intellectual knowledge to improve the mind.
  • Grit: Having strong courage and determination to face difficult challenges.
Diary of a CEO Politics & Economics

Interview 03: Pierre Poilievre: Why America Is Quietly Abandoning Its Allies (& What Comes Next)

Accessed Date: June 15, 2026 Source Page: The Diary Of A CEO YouTube Channel Link: View Content
Summary

The host of this interview is Steven Bartlett. He interviews Pierre Poilievre, a famous Canadian politician and the leader of the opposition in Canada. The main concept of this long interview covers world politics, especially the relationship between the USA and Canada.

They also discuss important economic and social issues like the housing crisis, inflation, and how Artificial Intelligence (AI) will change future jobs. Additionally, Pierre shares his emotional personal life story, including being adopted, his parents' divorce, and his experiences raising an autistic daughter.

My Opinion

I found this interview very interesting because it is rare to see a politician speak so openly and honestly about his personal life. His belief in "stoicism"—which means focusing only on what we can control in life—is a great lesson for everyone. Also, the discussion about how AI will disrupt the future job market was very eye-opening and informative for me.

Reflective Development - What You Learned
  • Pugnacious: Being quick to argue or fight for what you want.
  • Altruism: Caring about the happiness and well-being of other people.
  • Metastasize: To spread and grow dangerously out of control.
  • Rambunctious: Being full of energy and difficult to control.
  • Stoicism: The practice of focusing only on things you can control and staying calm during hard times.
